No medical exam life insurance allows people to apply for and obtain life insurance coverage without going through a traditional medical examination. It's designed for individuals who want coverage quickly, prefer to avoid the medical exam process, or may have health conditions that complicate traditional underwriting.

## Types of no-exam policies

**Simplified issue**: The applicant answers a health questionnaire (typically 5–15 questions about significant health conditions). No physical exam is required, but the insurer may review pharmacy records, motor vehicle records, or other data sources. Coverage limits are lower than fully underwritten policies.

**Guaranteed issue**: No health questions are asked and no exam is required. Virtually anyone within the eligible age range is accepted. Benefits are limited (typically $5,000–$25,000) and graded benefit periods often apply. Premium costs are higher per dollar of coverage.

**Accelerated underwriting**: A newer model that uses data analytics, medical records access, pharmacy databases, and algorithmic risk assessment to approve applications quickly — sometimes within minutes — without a physical exam. This can allow larger coverage amounts than simplified issue, at competitive rates.

## Tradeoffs

The elimination of the medical exam comes with tradeoffs:

- **Lower coverage limits**: Simplified issue typically caps around $500,000; guaranteed issue is usually much lower
- **Higher premiums**: The insurer carries more uncertainty without a full medical picture, and prices accordingly
- **Graded benefits**: Some guaranteed issue policies don't pay the full death benefit if death occurs in the first two years

## Who no-exam policies may be appropriate for

- People who have delayed getting coverage and want to act quickly
- Individuals with moderate health conditions who still want some coverage
- Those who prefer the convenience of a faster application process
- Older adults seeking final expense coverage

If you're in good health, a fully underwritten policy will typically deliver more coverage at lower cost.

# Gas Dryer Buying Guide for 2026

Gas dryers have one major advantage over electric dryers:

They tend to dry clothes faster while using less energy.

That's the good news.

The bad news is that shopping for a gas dryer comes with a few extra considerations—like making sure your laundry room actually has a gas connection and not accidentally turning a simple appliance purchase into a small remodeling project.

If you're considering a gas dryer in 2026, this guide will help you understand what matters, what doesn't, and how to find the right model for your home.

## Quick Answer

For most homeowners, the best gas dryer in 2026 will offer:

- Moisture-sensing technology
- 7.0–8.0 cubic feet of capacity
- Energy-efficient operation
- Wrinkle prevention features
- Steam cycles (optional)
- Reliable local service support

Top gas dryer brands for 2026 include:

- LG
- Electrolux
- Maytag
- Whirlpool

- Speed Queen

Gas dryers generally cost more upfront than electric models but often cost less to operate over time if natural gas rates are favorable.

## Why Choose a Gas Dryer?

Gas dryers generate heat using natural gas or propane while using electricity to power:

- Controls
- Drum motors
- Sensors
- Displays

Because gas heats more quickly than electric heating elements, many gas dryers can:

- Reach drying temperatures faster
- Reduce cycle times
- Lower long-term operating costs

For large families who run multiple loads each week, those savings can add up over time.

## Gas Dryer vs Electric Dryer

This is usually the first decision homeowners face.

| Feature | Gas Dryer | Electric Dryer |
|----------|------------|----------------|
| Purchase Price | Higher | Lower |
| Installation Cost | Higher | Lower |
| Drying Speed | Faster | Slower |
| Operating Cost | Usually Lower | Usually Higher |
| Utility Requirement | Gas + Electric | Electric Only |
| Availability | Good | Excellent |

For homes that already have a gas dryer hookup, the decision often comes down to operating costs and personal preference.

For homes without gas service in the laundry area, installation expenses can significantly change the equation.

## Who Should Buy a Gas Dryer?

A gas dryer may be a great choice if:

### You Already Have a Gas Hookup

This is the easiest scenario.

Replacing an existing gas dryer with another gas dryer is usually straightforward.

### You Do Lots of Laundry

Large households often benefit from:

- Faster drying cycles
- Lower operating costs
- Greater efficiency

### You Plan to Stay in Your Home

The longer you own the appliance, the more opportunity you have to benefit from lower operating costs.

### Energy Costs Matter

In many areas, natural gas remains less expensive than electricity for heating applications.

That can make gas dryers attractive for long-term ownership.

## Capacity Guide

Choosing the right size is one of the most important buying decisions.

### Small Households

1–2 people:

- 4.0–6.5 cubic feet

### Average Families

3–5 people:

- 7.0–8.0 cubic feet

### Large Families

5+ people:

- 8.0 cubic feet and above

For most households, a dryer between 7.3 and 7.8 cubic feet offers an excellent balance of capacity and efficiency.

It also provides enough room for:

- Comforters
- Bedding
- Towels
- Bulky items

Without forcing everything into a fabric traffic jam.

## Features Worth Paying For

Not every premium feature is marketing fluff.

Some genuinely improve performance and convenience.

### Moisture Sensors

If there's one feature you should prioritize, it's this.

Moisture sensors:

- Detect when clothes are dry
- Automatically stop the cycle
- Reduce energy use
- Prevent overdrying

This feature saves money and extends clothing life.

### Wrinkle Prevention

Wrinkle-prevention settings periodically tumble clothes after the cycle ends.

Benefits include:

- Fewer wrinkles
- Less ironing
- Reduced laundry guilt when you forget a load for three hours

### Steam Cycles

Steam options can help:

- Refresh clothes
- Remove odors
- Reduce wrinkles

Particularly useful for business attire and lightly worn garments.

### Sanitize Cycles

Many premium dryers now include sanitize settings designed to expose fabrics to higher temperatures.

These cycles can be useful for:

- Bedding
- Towels
- Children's clothing

### Reversible Doors

A surprisingly practical feature.

Depending on your laundry room layout, a reversible door can make daily use significantly more convenient.

## Features You Can Probably Skip

### Massive Touchscreens

A dryer's primary purpose remains:

Dry clothes.

If a model's touchscreen costs hundreds more without improving drying performance, think carefully before upgrading.

### Excessive Smart Features

Some smart functions are genuinely useful:

- Cycle notifications
- Maintenance reminders

Others mainly provide the ability to check your dryer from the couch.

Which, admittedly, is where most laundry management occurs.

### Dozens of Specialty Cycles

Most households regularly use:

- Normal
- Delicates
- Towels
- Bedding

A dryer offering 37 additional cycle options rarely changes your laundry routine.

## Installation Requirements

Gas dryers require more installation considerations than electric models.

### Gas Connection

You'll need:

- Natural gas or propane service
- Proper gas line connection
- Approved shutoff valve

### Electrical Outlet

Gas dryers still require standard electricity for controls and operation.

### Venting

Most gas dryers require:

- Exterior venting
- Proper duct installation

Poor venting can reduce efficiency and increase safety risks.

### Professional Installation

Many homeowners choose professional installation because gas connections must be properly installed and tested.

Saving money on installation isn't worth creating a safety issue.

## Operating Costs

One reason homeowners choose gas dryers is lower operating costs.

While utility rates vary by location, gas dryers often cost less to run than electric models.

Potential benefits include:

- Lower monthly utility costs
- Faster drying times
- Reduced energy consumption

The savings are usually most noticeable in households that run multiple loads each week.

## Maintenance Tips

Gas dryers require relatively little maintenance.

However, a few simple habits can improve performance.

### Clean the Lint Filter Every Load

This improves:

- Efficiency
- Drying speed
- Safety

### Inspect Venting Annually

Lint buildup can reduce airflow and create fire hazards.

### Monitor Drying Performance

Longer drying times often signal:

- Vent restrictions
- Sensor issues
- Airflow problems

Addressing these early can prevent larger repairs later.

## Common Buying Mistakes

### Buying the Wrong Capacity

Too small:

- Longer laundry days
- More loads

Too large:

- Higher purchase costs
- Potentially lower efficiency for small loads

### Ignoring Installation Costs

A gas dryer may cost more than expected if gas service must be added.

Always verify installation requirements first.

### Choosing Price Over Reliability

The cheapest model isn't always the best value.

Long-term reliability often matters more than saving a small amount upfront.

### Forgetting About Service Availability

Before purchasing a premium model, verify that local service providers support the brand.

Future-you will thank present-you.

## FAQ

### Are gas dryers better than electric dryers?

Neither is universally better. Gas dryers often dry faster and cost less to operate, while electric dryers typically cost less upfront and are easier to install.

### Do gas dryers save money?

In many areas, yes. Lower operating costs can offset higher purchase and installation expenses over time.

### How long does a gas dryer last?

Most gas dryers last approximately 10–15 years with proper maintenance.

### Are gas dryers safe?

Yes, when properly installed and maintained. Professional installation is strongly recommended.

### What size gas dryer should I buy?

Most households benefit from a dryer between 7.0 and 8.0 cubic feet.

## Final Thoughts

A gas dryer can be an excellent investment for homeowners who already have a gas hookup and want faster drying performance with potentially lower operating costs.

For most buyers in 2026, the sweet spot includes:

- Moisture sensors
- 7.0–8.0 cubic feet of capacity
- Wrinkle prevention
- Strong reliability ratings
- Local service support

The best gas dryer isn't necessarily the one with the most features.

It's the one that dries clothes efficiently, lasts for years, and doesn't require a user manual the size of a college textbook to start a load of towels.

And if your current dryer sounds like it's trying to launch into orbit every time it runs, this may be the year for an upgrade.

# Homeowners Insurance

**Plain-English definition:** Homeowners insurance is a policy that helps pay for damage to your home, personal belongings, and certain liability claims when covered events occur. It is different from a home warranty, which covers the repair or replacement of certain home systems and appliances that fail from normal wear and tear.

## Quick Answer

Homeowners insurance protects against covered risks such as fire, windstorms, theft, vandalism, and liability claims. A home warranty helps cover repairs to household systems and appliances when they break down due to everyday use. Most homeowners benefit from understanding both because they serve very different purposes.

## Homeowners Insurance Definition in Home Warranty Terms

When discussing home warranties, homeowners insurance often comes up because many people mistakenly assume the two products provide the same type of protection.

They do not.

Homeowners insurance is designed to help cover sudden and accidental damage caused by covered perils. Depending on the policy, this may include:

- Fire and smoke damage
- Wind and hail damage
- Theft and vandalism
- Certain water damage events
- Liability claims involving injuries on your property
- Temporary living expenses if the home becomes uninhabitable

A home warranty, on the other hand, is a service contract that helps cover the repair or replacement of certain home systems and appliances when they fail due to normal wear and tear.

For example, homeowners insurance might help if lightning damages your electrical system. A home warranty may help if that same electrical system stops working because components wear out over time.

Understanding this distinction is essential when evaluating a [home warranty](/home-warranty/).

## Why Homeowners Insurance Matters for Homeowners

For most homeowners, insurance is one of the most important financial protections they have.

Without insurance, a major fire, severe storm, or theft could result in significant out-of-pocket expenses.

Homeowners insurance matters because it can help protect:

- The structure of your home
- Detached structures such as garages and sheds
- Personal belongings
- Personal liability exposure
- Additional living expenses after a covered loss

At the same time, homeowners should understand that insurance policies are not maintenance plans. They generally do not cover the gradual breakdown of systems and appliances.

That's where home warranties may provide additional protection.

## Example Scenario

Imagine a homeowner named Mike.

One summer, a lightning strike causes a power surge that damages parts of his home's electrical system.

Because the damage resulted from a covered event, his homeowners insurance policy may help cover repairs after any deductible is paid.

A year later, Mike's aging air conditioner fails because the compressor wears out after years of use.

That situation is generally not an insurance claim because there was no covered peril. If Mike has [HVAC coverage](/coverage/hvac/) through a home warranty plan, the warranty may help cover the repair or replacement subject to contract terms, exclusions, and coverage limits.

The same house experienced two different problems, but each required a different type of protection.

## How Homeowners Insurance Affects Home Warranty Coverage

Homeowners insurance and home warranties often complement each other.

Insurance typically handles:

- Sudden accidental damage
- Weather-related losses
- Fire damage
- Theft
- Liability claims

Home warranties typically focus on:

- Mechanical failures
- Electrical failures caused by wear and tear
- Appliance breakdowns
- Aging systems that stop working during normal use

Examples of systems and appliances often covered by home warranties include:

- [HVAC coverage](/coverage/hvac/)
- [Plumbing coverage](/coverage/plumbing/)
- [Electrical coverage](/coverage/electrical/)
- [Water Heater Coverage](/coverage/water-heater/)
- [Kitchen Appliances](/coverage/kitchen-appliances/)
- [Laundry Appliances](/coverage/laundry-appliances/)

Many homeowners choose to have both because each addresses different risks.

Home warranty coverage varies by provider, plan, location, and contract terms. Always read the contract before buying.

## What to Watch Out For

One of the biggest homeowner mistakes is assuming homeowners insurance covers everything.

It doesn't.

### Normal Wear and Tear

Insurance generally does not pay for equipment that simply wears out over time.

An old dishwasher that stops working usually isn't an insurance claim.

### Maintenance Issues

Most policies require homeowners to properly maintain their property.

Damage caused by neglect may not be covered.

### Coverage Limits

Insurance policies often contain specific coverage limits for certain categories of property.

Likewise, home warranties have their own [coverage limits](/glossary/coverage-limit/).

### Exclusions

Every policy contains exclusions.

Understanding an insurance policy's exclusions is just as important as reviewing home warranty [exclusions](/glossary/exclusion/).

### Deductibles

Insurance claims often require homeowners to pay a deductible before coverage applies.

Home warranties typically use a [service call fee](/glossary/service-call-fee/) instead.

## Homeowners Insurance vs Related Concepts

### Homeowners Insurance vs Home Warranty

This is the comparison that causes the most confusion.

Homeowners insurance covers covered perils such as fire, theft, and storms.

A home warranty helps cover breakdowns caused by normal wear and tear.

Many homeowners benefit from having both.

### Homeowners Insurance vs Coverage Limit

A [coverage limit](/glossary/coverage-limit/) is the maximum amount a policy or warranty may pay for a covered loss or claim.

Homeowners insurance policies and home warranties both contain coverage limits.

### Homeowners Insurance vs Exclusion

An [exclusion](/glossary/exclusion/) identifies situations that are not covered.

Even comprehensive insurance policies contain exclusions that can affect claim outcomes.

### Homeowners Insurance vs Pre-Existing Condition

Home warranties often restrict coverage for [pre-existing conditions](/glossary/pre-existing-condition/).

Insurance policies also have rules regarding existing damage and undisclosed conditions.

## FAQ

### Is homeowners insurance the same as a home warranty?

No. Homeowners insurance covers certain accidental losses and property damage from covered events, while a home warranty covers certain systems and appliances that break down due to normal wear and tear.

### Does homeowners insurance cover appliance breakdowns?

Usually not when the appliance simply wears out over time. Insurance generally covers damage resulting from covered perils rather than routine mechanical failures.

### Do mortgage lenders require homeowners insurance?

In most cases, yes. Mortgage lenders typically require homeowners insurance to protect their financial interest in the property.

### Does a home warranty replace homeowners insurance?

No. A home warranty is not a substitute for homeowners insurance. The two products serve different purposes and cover different risks.

### What kinds of damage are typically covered by homeowners insurance?

Coverage often includes losses caused by fire, windstorms, hail, theft, vandalism, and certain liability claims, depending on the policy.

### Why do some homeowners buy both homeowners insurance and a home warranty?

Because they address different risks. Insurance helps protect against covered disasters and liability claims, while a home warranty may help with the cost of repairing or replacing covered systems and appliances.

## Final Thoughts

Homeowners insurance is designed to protect your home, belongings, and finances from covered losses such as fire, storms, theft, and liability claims. A home warranty serves a different purpose by helping cover the repair or replacement of certain systems and appliances that fail due to normal wear and tear. Understanding the difference can help homeowners avoid coverage surprises and build a more complete protection strategy for their home.
